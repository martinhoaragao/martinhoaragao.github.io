    Jack of all trades

      1.71 minutes read

    Heyo!

    So, it seems you’ve arrived on the dark side of the web! Oh wait, is it just where you get information about me? Bah, just a second, let me clean this up then.

    As you could see, this is my tiny corner of the internet, as such I feel entitled to loosen up on the language. I find it quite hard to both describe myself and what I do easily yet, in a few words, here goes:

    • Annoying
    • Curious
    • Dynamic
    • Engineer

    To be honest, I’m only 100% sure on the first point, but certainly the most valuable is the third. Despite being fairly young (clearly an understatement), I’ve successfully tried out a myriad of things, all of which enhanced what I do.

    Although I don’t intend to go into much detail about everything I truly identify myself with the following expression:

    Jack of all trades, master of none,
    Certainly better than a master of one.

    Fun fact: This figure of speech through the times keeps shifting from positive to negative. “Jack of all trades” - Ambiguous. “Master of none” - According to wikipedia was added later. “Certainly better than a master of one” - Recovers the flattering.

    Anyway, for all I care, it is by bringing together different areas that new ones are created. In sum I’m making a fuss because I don’t feel satisfied by being either just a dev or just a manager.

    So, here you go:

    1. Developer
    2. Manager
    3. Speaker
    4. Entrepreneur

    I certainly owe it to Young Minho Enterprise for the second, to CeSIUM for the first and, to both of them, for the third. Funny though, as an entrepreneur I’ve do it all, and more! That’s where Nutrium came in. Right now, I’m focused on the first and partially the second sections.

    All in all, I find it hard to decline a challenge where I can grow. What is there to lose?

    “Those who stand on top of a mountain did not fall there.” Adapted from Vincent Lombardi
